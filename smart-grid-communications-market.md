<p><ul><li>Wired Communications System</li><li>Wireless Communications System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Smart Grid Communications encompasses two primary market types: Wired and Wireless Communications Systems. Wired systems utilize physical cables, such as fiber optics and copper lines, offering reliable, high-capacity data transfer essential for monitoring and control. In contrast, Wireless Communications Systems leverage technologies like cellular, satellite, and mesh networks, enabling flexibility and quick deployment in areas lacking infrastructure. Both systems play critical roles in enhancing grid efficiency, supporting advanced metering, demand response, and real-time data exchange between utilities and consumers.</p></p>
