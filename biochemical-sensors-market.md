<p><p>The biochemical sensors market encompasses several types, each utilizing different mechanisms for detection. Electrochemical sensors measure current or voltage changes due to biochemical reactions, making them ideal for detecting specific ions or molecules. Thermal sensors assess temperature variations resulting from reactions, useful in monitoring metabolic processes. Piezoelectric sensors detect mass changes or mechanical stress through frequency variations, while optical sensors analyze light intensity changes due to interactions with biomolecules. Each type caters to various applications in healthcare, food safety, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
