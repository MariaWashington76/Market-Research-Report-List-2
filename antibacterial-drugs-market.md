<p><p>The antibacterial drugs market is categorized into several types, including cephalosporins, penicillins, fluoroquinolones, macrolides, and carbapenems. Cephalosporins are broad-spectrum antibiotics effective against various bacterial infections. Penicillins are among the oldest antibiotics, primarily targeting Gram-positive bacteria. Fluoroquinolones are potent, broad-spectrum agents often used for respiratory and urinary tract infections. Macrolides are effective against respiratory pathogens and have a unique mechanism. Carbapenems are reserved for severe infections due to their effectiveness against multi-drug-resistant bacteria. Other categories include various emerging antibiotics and combinations.</p></p>
